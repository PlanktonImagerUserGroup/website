About the Group

The plankton imager is a flow‑through camera system that continuously photographs plankton in seawater pumped from a research vessel. It captures thousands of colour images per minute, covering organisms from roughly 0.2 mm to several centimetres. Typical sampling rates are about 34 litres of water per minute, generating tens of gigabytes of image data each day. This allows near real‑time monitoring of plankton communities at sea without manual sample collection. Data can be processed automatically to classify plankton and provide rapid summaries for ecological assessments.

The Plankton Imager User Group (Pi‑10) is a collaborative community focused on advancing automated plankton imaging. It brings together users to share knowledge, develop best practices, and harmonise workflows for data processing and analysis. The group works on improving machine learning tools, standardising protocols, and supporting integration of imaging outputs into monitoring frameworks. Regular discussions and shared resources help members troubleshoot challenges and innovate collectively. Its aim is to ensure consistent, high-quality use of Pi‑10 technology across diverse applications.
